    August 2024 - February 2025

    Assigned to a collaborative research project with Hokkaido University Veterinary Medical Center. I was responsible for improving and implementing an AI model for bone removal from animal X-ray images. While bone removal in human X-ray images typically requires paired images ("with bone" and "without bone"), veterinary medicine faces the challenge that obtaining bone-removed images in clinical settings is difficult, making it impossible to acquire ground truth data for training. To address this constraint, I worked on designing and implementing a bone removal AI model that fuses other medical imaging modalities with X-ray images and functions even in situations where supervisory data is lacking. This research has high clinical significance for supporting veterinary diagnosis and presents significant challenges in image processing and machine learning research.
